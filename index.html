Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

(Missile Man Of India)

Life Of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam
  • 1931: Born on 15 October 1931 to a Tamil Muslim family in the pilgrimage centre of Rameswaram on Pamban Island.
  • 1954: He moved to University of Madras from where he graduated in Physic.
  • 1955: He moved to Madras to study "Aerospaca Engineering" in Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960: After graduating from engineering Kalam joined the 'Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation' as Defence Research & Development Service(DRDS).
  • 1969: Kalam was transferred to 'Indian Space Research Organisation(ISRO)' where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the 'Rohini Satellite' in near-earth orbit in July 1980.
  • 1970's: kalam also directed two projects,Project Devil and Project Valliant which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV programme.
  • 1970-1990's: Kalam made effort to develop the "Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV)" and SLV-III projects,both of which proved to be successful.
  • 1980's: Kalam was appointed as 'Chief Executive for Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme(IGDMP)'. Missiles under mission including 'Agni an intermediate range ballastic missile' and 'Prithvi tactical surface-to-surface misslie'
  • 1992-1999: Kalam served as "Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation."
  • 11-13 May 1998: The 'Pokhran-II' tests were series of 'Five Nuclear bomb test explosions hy Indian Army's Pokhran Test Range' under American Satellite and it was successfully tested.
  • 2002-2007: Kalam served as 11th President Of India.
  • 2013: Kalam was recipient of the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society
  • 27 July 2015: Kalam was died while travelled to shilong Meghalaya while Climbing flight stairs experinced discomfort.
